Exploring South Sinai: Tourism, Heritage, and Communities

Welcome to South Sinai Governorate

South Sinai Governorate is a region rich in history and cultural heritage, nestled between the Red Sea and the deserts of Egypt. Its strategic location has long been a crossroads of civilizations.

The governorate features unique key landmarks such as ancient monasteries, Bedouin traditions, and vibrant markets. It offers an experience that blends history and modern tourism.

Natural landscapes here include spectacular mountain ranges, pristine beaches with coral reefs, and stunning desert vistas, creating an unparalleled setting for tourists.

Project Overview

Population of South Sinai

South Sinai is one of the least densely populated governorates in Egypt. The population is relatively small and is mainly concentrated in a few urban centers such as El Tor (the capital), Sharm El-Sheikh, Dahab, and Nuweiba. The region is also home to Bedouin tribes who preserve a unique cultural and social heritage.

Tourism in South Sinai

Tourism is a vital sector in South Sinai, significantly contributing to the local and national economy. The region is known for its stunning natural beauty, including desert landscapes, coral reefs, and pristine beaches, as well as its religious and historical significance—especially in areas like Saint Catherine.

Tourist Attractions

  • Sharm El-Sheikh: A world-famous resort city known for diving and luxury tourism.
  • Dahab: Popular for eco-tourism and water sports.
  • Saint Catherine Monastery: A UNESCO World Heritage Site of great religious importance.
  • Ras Mohammed National Park: A protected marine area with rich biodiversity.
  • Mount Sinai: A major pilgrimage site and popular hiking destination.

Tourist Statistics

South Sinai attracts millions of visitors annually, both domestic and international. The number of tourists varies depending on global travel trends and regional stability, but Sharm El-Sheikh alone often receives over one million tourists per year, making it one of the top destinations in the Middle East.
